About me

I am a neuroscientist working as a postdoctoral associate at Scripps Research/HHMI, supervised by Li Ye. My PhD research focuses on the neural circuit mechanisms underlying the cognitive regulation of feeding behavior, as well as the development of novel computational and experimental tools for analyzing neural activity and behavior. My broader academic interest lies in integrating approaches from neurobiology and computational modeling to explore how the brain supports complex behavior and cognitive functions.

Prior to Scripps, I completed my undergraduate studies in Biological Science at Tsinghua University, China. My undergraduate thesis was supervised by Yi Zhong, focusing on the cortical circuits for observational learning in mice.

Outside the lab, I enjoy birding, photography, table tennis, and playing Chinese flute (and I am teaching myself piano now!). I'm also an amateur entomologist with a particular interest in the taxonomy of Odonata (dragonflies and damselflies).
