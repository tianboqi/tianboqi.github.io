About me

I am a PhD candidate in Neuroscience at Scripps Research, advised by Li Ye. My thesis research focuses on the neural circuitry at the interplay between metabolism and behavior. Broadly speaking, my interest lies in using tools spanning from neurobiology to computational modeling to study the neural circuits for behavior and cognition.

Before Scripps, I was an undergraduate student in Biological Science at Tsinghua University, China. My undergrad thesis was advised by Yi Zhong, focusing on the neural circuits for observational learning in mice. I visited Dayu Lin's lab at NYU in my junior year, studying the limbic circuits for social behaviors in rodents.

Outside the lab, I enjoy birding, photographing, playing table tennis, and playing Chinese flute (and I am teaching myself piano now!). I'm also an ameteur in the entomology and taxonomy of Odonata (dragonflies and damselflies).
