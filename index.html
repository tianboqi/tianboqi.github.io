About me

I am a PhD candidate in Neuroscience at Scripps Research, supervised by Li Ye, an HHMI investigator. My research focuses on the neural circuit mechanisms underlying the cognitive regulation of food intake, alongside the development of novel computational and experimental tools for analyzing neural activity and behavior. My broader academic interest lies in integrating approaches from neurobiology and computational modeling to explore how the brain supports complex behavior and cognitive functions.

Prior to Scripps, I completed my undergraduate studies in Biological Science at Tsinghua University, China. My undergrad thesis was advised by Yi Zhong, focusing on the cortical circuits for observational learning in mice. Additionally, I volunteered in Dayu Lin's lab at NYU, studying the limbic circuits for social behaviors in rodents.

Outside the lab, I enjoy birding, photographing, playing table tennis, and playing Chinese flute (and I am teaching myself piano now!). I'm also an ameteur in the entomology and taxonomy of Odonata (dragonflies and damselflies).
