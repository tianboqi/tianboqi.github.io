About me

I am a PhD candidate in Neuroscience at Scripps Research, advised by Li Ye. My research focuses on the neural circuit mechanism underlying the cognitive regulation of feeding behaviors, meanwhile also developing novel tools for neural circuit studies. My broader academic interest lies in integrating approaches from neurobiology and computational modeling to explore how the brain supports complex behavior and cognitive functions.

Prior to Scripps, I completed my undergraduate studies in Biological Science at Tsinghua University, China. My undergrad thesis was advised by Yi Zhong, focusing on the cortical circuits for observational learning in mice. Additionally, I visited Dayu Lin's lab at NYU in my junior year, studying the limbic circuits for social behaviors in rodents.

Outside the lab, I enjoy birding, photographing, playing table tennis, and playing Chinese flute (and I am teaching myself piano now!). I'm also an ameteur in the entomology and taxonomy of Odonata (dragonflies and damselflies).
